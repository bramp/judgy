Privacy Policy

Effective date: 9 March 2026

Grids is a puzzle game made with love. We want you to have fun without worrying about your data — so here's the full story, no fine print tricks.

1. What We Collect (and don't)

Game Saves (strictly necessary)

We remember which levels you've unlocked, your solutions, and your settings — all stored right on your device. It never phones home. Think of it like a bookmark, but for puzzles.

Analytics (optional, pinky-promise)

If you opt in, we use Firebase Analytics to peek at anonymous stats like:

  • Which levels get played the most
  • How long a level takes to crack
  • How many attempts before that aha! moment

This helps us spot puzzles that are unfairly hard (or suspiciously easy). We never collect your name, email, or anything that identifies you personally.

Under the hood, Firebase Analytics may collect device identifiers and IP addresses as described in Google's Firebase privacy documentation. IP addresses are only used for rough geolocation and aren't kept around long.

2. You're in Charge

The first time you open the game, we'll ask if you'd like to share anonymous stats. No pressure! You can flip your choice any time in Settings. Analytics stays off until you say otherwise.

3. Sharing? Nope.

We don't sell, rent, or hand your data to anyone. Analytics data is processed by Google Firebase under their data processing terms — that's it.

4. How Long We Keep Things

Game saves live on your device until you clear storage or uninstall. Firebase Analytics data sticks around per Google's retention policies (14 months by default for event data, then poof).

5. Kids & Privacy

Grids isn't aimed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect data from them.

6. Future Updates

If we tweak this policy, the date at the top of the page will change so you'll know.

7. Say Hi

Got questions? Spotted something weird? Open an issue on the project's GitHub repository — we'd love to hear from you.
